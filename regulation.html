Understanding the
Triple Regulatory Architecture

UEMOA — Monetary Union

Activities Regulated by the BCEAO

UEMOA tightly governs financial activities across 8 member states through the BCEAO and regional market authorities.

  • Banks, financial institutions and microfinance (mandatory BCEAO licence)
  • Financial markets — BRVM and regional securities
  • Payment systems and electronic money transfers
  • Insurance and reinsurance operations
  • VAT harmonisation and customs duty framework
  • Public procurement, concessions and quality standards
  • Telecommunications, energy and regional infrastructure
ECOWAS — Economic Community

Regionally Regulated Activities

ECOWAS governs cross-border activities and trade integration across 15 member states, complementing UEMOA's monetary framework.

  • Free movement of goods, services and capital (Protocol A/P1/1/03)
  • Road, maritime and air transport — safety and transit standards
  • Energy exchanges: electricity, hydrocarbons and mining
  • Monetary policy harmonisation with UEMOA
  • Anti-money laundering and counter-terrorism financing
  • Cross-border telecommunications and cybersecurity
  • Personal data protection across member states
